The maritime industry has long understood physical security through the International Ship and Port Facility Security (ISPS) Code. However, the digitization of navigation, cargo management, and engine control systems has introduced cyber vulnerabilities that traditional ship security plans may not address.

## Beyond ISPS

Effective maritime security today requires:

- **Ship Security Plans** that reference cyber incident procedures
- **Crew training** on both physical threat recognition and phishing awareness
- **Port coordination** that shares threat intelligence across digital and physical domains

## Practical Steps

Shipping companies should conduct integrated assessments that evaluate:

- Bridge system network exposure
- Crew access control and visitor management
- Emergency communication redundancy
- Coordination with port facility security officers

Security is not a checkbox—it is an operational capability that protects crew, cargo, and continuity of operations.
